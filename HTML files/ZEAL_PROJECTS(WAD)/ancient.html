Ancient India

The foundation of Indian civilization and culture.

Indus Valley Civilization

The Indus Valley Civilization (3300–1300 BCE) was one of the earliest urban cultures, with well-planned cities, advanced drainage systems, and flourishing trade. It laid the foundation of Indian society, art, and craft, influencing later civilizations.

Mauryan Empire

The Mauryan Empire (321–185 BCE) unified much of India. Emperor Ashoka promoted Buddhism and moral governance, leaving inscriptions called the Edicts of Ashoka. The empire was known for efficient administration, roads, trade, and cultural development.
