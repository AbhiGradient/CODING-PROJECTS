"A concise guide to the wonders of our Solar System"

1. Mercury:

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest planet in the Solar System. Its surface is covered with craters, and it has almost no atmosphere. A day on Mercury lasts 59 Earth days, and its temperature varies drastically between extreme heat and freezing cold.



2. Venus:

Venus is similar in size to Earth but has a thick, toxic atmosphere primarily composed of carbon dioxide, creating a runaway greenhouse effect. Surface temperatures can reach up to 465°C, making it the hottest planet in the Solar System. Its rotation is slow and retrograde, opposite to most planets.



3. Earth:

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to support life. It has a rich atmosphere with oxygen and water, enabling diverse ecosystems. The planet rotates on its axis every 24 hours and revolves around the Sun in 365.25 days.

4. Mars & 5. Jupiter:

  1. Mars:

    Mars, the Red Planet, is famous for its iron oxide surface giving it a reddish hue. It has the tallest volcano and the deepest canyon in the Solar System. Scientists are interested in Mars for potential past or present microbial life.


  2. Jupiter:

    Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System, a gas giant with a massive atmosphere mainly of hydrogen and helium. It has the famous Great Red Spot, a gigantic storm, and dozens of moons including Ganymede, the largest moon in the Solar System.
